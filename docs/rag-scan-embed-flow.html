Athena Knowledge Base — Scan & Embed, Explained

How a help-center URL becomes a searchable knowledge base, why the cost is shown before embedding, and why Gemini is called twice in the pipeline (once during scan, once during embed).

The big picture

Turning your docs into something the bot can search happens in two separate phases, with a human confirmation in between. This split exists for one reason: embedding costs money, so we measure the work first, show you the price, and only spend after you say "go".

1 · ScanFetch + split pages into chunks. Phase A
2 · EstimateCount tokens → compute embedding $ cost.
3 · ConfirmAdmin reviews cost & clicks Embed.
4 · EmbedTurn chunks into vectors. Phase B
5 · ReadyBot can now retrieve & answer.

Two different Gemini models are involved

gemini-2.0-flash
chat / LLM		 Writes a 1–2 sentence context header summarizing each page (and later, writes the bot's answers). context-builder.service.ts, called from indexer.scanPage() during scan
gemini-embedding-001

This is the key thing to understand: "scanning" and "embedding" are not the same Gemini call. Scanning currently uses the chat model (for the summary); embedding uses the embeddings model (for the vectors).

Phase A — Scan SCANNING

Triggered by POST /kb/scan/start. A background worker crawls the site and, for every page, runs indexer.scanPage(). No vectors are created yet.

Strip nav/footer/script, convert HTML → Markdown, then split into sections on H2/H3 headings. Tiny sections are merged; huge ones are split. Each chunk records its heading path, a deep-link URL with #anchor, and an estimated tokenCount.

⏸ Confirmation gate AWAITING_CONFIRM

The UI shows N pages · M chunks · ≈ $X to embed and waits. Nothing else happens until the admin clicks Embed now (POST /kb/embed/confirm) or Cancel (which deletes the SCANNED rows and returns to IDLE). This is the "show cost before embedding" guarantee.

Triggered by POST /kb/embed/confirm. A worker walks every SCANNED source and calls indexer.embedSource().

Now the bot can answer. At question time the same embeddings model turns the customer's question into a vector, and retrieval finds the chunks whose vectors are closest (plus a keyword search), then gemini-2.0-flash writes the answer from those chunks.

Why is Gemini used during scanning?

This is the part that looks surprising — scanning is supposed to be "just fetch pages", yet it calls the LLM. The reason is a retrieval-quality technique called contextual retrieval.

The problem it solves

A single chunk often loses the context of the page it came from. A chunk that says "Pro includes 50 accounts per connector" doesn't contain the words "pricing" or "plan comparison". So a search for "Pro plan limits" might miss it — the chunk is correct but orphaned from its page's topic.

The fix: a context header

Before embedding, ask gemini-2.0-flash for a one-line summary of the whole page and glue it to the front of every chunk. Now each chunk's vector also carries the page's topic, so it's findable.

chunk text actually embedded:

[CONTEXT: Two Minute Reports pricing page comparing the Lite, Basic, Pro
          and Business plans — users, accounts per connector, and pricing.]
### Pro — $129 / month
* 10 users
* 50 accounts per connector
* Priority support
...
# the [CONTEXT] line is generated by Gemini during SCAN (step 4),
# then embedded into the vector during EMBED (step 2).

The trade-off (and what the improvement plan changes)

Because the context header is generated per page during the scan, the scan makes one Gemini chat call for every page — that is the main reason the initial scan feels slow, and it also means a small Gemini charge is incurred before the "confirm cost" step (the displayed estimate only counts embedding cost).

The planned optimization keeps the technique but moves the context-header generation into Phase B (right before embedding) and fetches pages in parallel. Scanning becomes pure fetch + chunk (genuinely fast and free), the quality benefit of the context header is unchanged, and the cost gate still happens before any spend.

State machine (kbPhase)

IDLE→ SCANNING→ AWAITING_CONFIRM→ EMBEDDING→ DONE

Cancel during scan or at the confirm gate deletes the SCANNED rows and returns to IDLE/CANCELLED. The whole machine is driven by fields on AppConfig and polled by the settings UI every few seconds.
